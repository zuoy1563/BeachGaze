Water Quality

In the following graph, the blue line indicates the historic results of bacteria contained in per 100mL sea water, while the red line indicates the recommended upper limit of enterococci. If a point has value of 0, it does not mean that there is no bacteria in the sea water, but there is only few bacteria so that it cannot be observed clearly.

Water quality is an important indicator for you to decide if the beach is suitable for you to take your children swimming or not.

EPA assess short-term water quality by comparing results against a dry weather enterococci trigger (>400 org/100mL). Twice daily forecasts predict likelihood of stormwater pollution during and after rain that may cause high enterococci levels.

It can be seen that most of the time, the water quality is quite good. However, there are some exceptions to the norm so it is best to check the most recent water quality results before visiting the beach.
