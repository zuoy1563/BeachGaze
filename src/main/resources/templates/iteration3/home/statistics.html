Drowning

By examining the following graphs, it is understood that there are not a lot of drowning cases in Victoria, and most of them are non-fatal.
However, it should be noted that the number as well as the percentage of fatal incidents that happened around natural water are much higher than the incidents that happened at other locations. As a result, it is important for you to take good care of your children while at beaches.


Melanoma

Melanoma is the most fatal skin cancer and the main cause of it is sunburn, which means that you should pay more attention to protect yourself and your children from sunburn.

1 out of 8 adults are sunburnt on average summer weekend.
1 out of 5 teenagers are sunburnt on average summer weekend.
Sunburn causes 95% of melanomas, the most fatal skin cancer.

It can be understood from the following graph that Victoria holds the third largest number of people who suffer from Melanoma. So it is important for you to read UV Instructions before visiting beaches with your children.
Note: The data for the number of Melanoma incidents in New South Wales in 2014 is missing.
